    BASIC FEATURES OF THE DATA

    Before diving into the challenge, I take a quick look at the data.

    1. What's the size of the dataset? We have cell phone traces associated with two malls. There are 9,195 traces for Mall G and 50,294 traces from Mall S collected over a period of two weeks, from Jan 13th - Jan 26th, 2014.

    2. How many unique users are there? The Mall G and Mall S datasets have 5,959 and 33,797 unique users, respectively.

    3. What fraction of users visit the malls only once in the two-week period? Most users visit the malls just once. Mall G and Mall S received a total of 85% and 76.8% unique visits, respectively (see figure below).


     

    4. How often do frequent visitors go to the mall? In Mall S, 64.2% of frequent visitors appear at the mall exactly twice. In Mall G, only 47.9% of frequent users are at the mall exactly twice, so users are nearly equally likely to go to mall G twice or more frequently. But some users visited the mall quite often in the two-week period: 74 users appeared at mall G over 10 times, while 236 do so at mall S. This could be a hint into who works at each mall.

    5. How much time do visitors spend at the mall? The median visit duration for Malls G and S are 1.1 hrs and 1.6 hrs, respectively. The average for Malls G and S is 1.4 hrs and 1.9 hrs. Because the mean is more strongly affected by outliers, we expect to see a right-skewed distribution of visit durations in both malls.

    6. How many events per trace are most typical? Between 2 and 10 events. In rare cases, traces have over 100 events.

    7. Are there any users that visit both Malls? The users at Mall G and Mall S are different and must be treated independently. Even though 418 user IDs are present in both datasets, their residency zip codes don't match, and therefore we can't assume a connection between them.



    WHEN DO USERS GO TO THE MALL?

    Instinctively, I would expect most people to go to the mall on Saturday. But what does the data say?

    Indeed, Mall S has more clients on Saturday, but that that's not the case for Mall G. There, as many users visit the mall on Satuday as on any other day of the week, roughly speaking.

    BIASES

    The data for mall G is smaller, we only have traces for one cell phone company. It's possible that the service is better in the area of the mall for this company.
